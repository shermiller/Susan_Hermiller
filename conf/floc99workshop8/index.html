FLoC'99 Workshop
Gröbner Bases and Rewriting Techniques
June 30 - July 1, 1999, Trento, Italy

This workshop preliminary to the RTA conference brought together people from various fields of mathematics and computer science, all having a common interest in symbolic rewriting techniques. The connections between methods from Gröbner basis theory and string rewriting were particularly highlighted.

Tutorials

  • B. Reinert (Universität Kaiserslautern), Tutorial on Gröbner bases in monoid and group rings
  • S. Hermiller (University of Nebraska Lincoln), Tutorial on string rewriting systems and extensions of Gröbner bases
  • B. Keller (University of Montana), Tutorial on implementing Gröbner basis methods

Workshop Sections

  • U. Martin, D. Shand (University of St Andrews), Invariants, patterns and weights for ordering terms
  • D. Hofbauer, Ch. Koegl, K. Madlener, F. Otto, B. Reinert (Gesamthochschule Kassel and Universität Kaiserslautern), XSSR: an experimental system for string-rewriting - decision problems, algorithms, and implementation
  • W. de Graaf (University of St Andrews), Hall and Gröbner bases and rewriting in free Lie algebras
  • A. Heyworth (University of Wales), Rewriting procedures generalise to Kan extensions of actions of categories
  • P. Nordbeck (Lund University), Non-commutative Gröbner bases under composition
  • J. Mansson (Lund University), On linear equations in some non-commutative algebras
  • S. Pride (University of Glasgow), Rewriting systems from a geometric viewpoint

Organizing Committee
